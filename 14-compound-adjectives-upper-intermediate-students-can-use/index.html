Most frequent hyphenated compound adjectives

ADJECTIVES, B2, compound, range, vocabulary

Here’s a sentence with 3 examples of hyphenated compound adjectives:

  • The so-called experts put her in long-term care because of her short-term memory.

There are pages around on the internet that list English Vocabulary Profile lists that seem to be used as what should be taught at a particular level.  This is always going to be a slightly controversial approach.  Since the EVP is about what past students could do, not what future students should do.  These lists do help possibly triangulate what vocabulary used is most frequently with what students can do at a given level.

In the English Grammar Profile, B2 point 60 in the category of  ADJECTIVES is defined as

“combining, … an increasing range of compound adjectives.”

However its examples include: ‘up-to-date‘ which is B1 on English Vocabulary Profile, and ‘state-of-the-art” is listed as C1.  “user-friendly” is not listed on the EVP at all.  This then means there is an unclear clash between vocabulary and grammar levels.   For the purposes which are more about vocabulary than grammar, and where we need more examples to explore, it is best to rely on the 14 hyphenated adjectives that the EVP offers at B2:

 bad-tempered grown-up hi-tech last-minute long-distance long-term non-smoking self-confident short-term so-called well-balanced well-built well-organized well-paid
