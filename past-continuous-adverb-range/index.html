past continuous + adverb (range)

A2, ADVERBS, B1, B2, C2, continuous, PAST

This is another grammar point where the English Vocabulary Profile is probably better at profiling the language than the English Grammar Profile.  There are a handful of adverbs that already make the task across levels quite difficult to follow.  We then should consider the hundred other adverbs that appear in this ‘mid position’.

A2 point 7 in the category of PAST:

with a limited range of adverbs in the normal mid position.

For example:

I was just killing time waiting for Griffin.

listen

The A2 examples in the EGP:

  • always
  • just

However, ‘always’ is also listed at C2 with ‘constantly’ for negative repeated actions.

This is too hard to find automatically and when we read texts manually, it can also be ambiguous whether it is negative or neutral:

TLC STUDENT SPEAKING TEST EXAMPLE:

Under colonial rule,

the British and the Chinese were always mixing their own cultures together.

male China B1

B1 point 49 in the category of PAST:

past continuous with an increasing range of adverbs in the normal mid position.

EGP examples contain:

  • still
  • actually

B1 Point 26 in the category of past/ continuous

ongoing repeated events in the past, often with ‘always’

B2 Point 70 in the category of PAST is defined as:

past continuous with a wide range of adverbs in the normal mid position.

The first EGP example for B2 is clearly an example of the C2 grammar mentioned earlier:

My younger brother was constantly bothering me.

Note that all of these will overlap a range of adverbs.
