present perfect continuous

B1, B2, CONJUNCTIONS, continuous, FOCUS, PAST, perfect, PRESENT

The following sentence means that you have a question that you wanted or intended to ask someone for a long time, but you did not ask it until now.   It is a way to introduce a question.

I‘ve been meaning to ask you something.

listen

There are 3 B1 basic present perfect continuous points in the category of PAST in the English Grammar Profile that are hard to automatically differentiate from 1 basic point at B2.

B1 point 19 is defined:

present perfect continuous: repeated activities that began in the past and are still continuing.

B1 point 42:

present perfect continuous: AFFIRMATIVE

B1 point 45:

present perfect continuous:  a single activity or state that began in the past and is still continuing, often with ‘for’ or ‘since’.

B2 Point 71 is:

the present perfect continuous to focus on a finished activity in the recent past but where the effects or results are still important or relevant.

_jj _cs * have|has been _vvg
