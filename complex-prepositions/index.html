complex prepositions

A2, B1, B2, C1, complex, formal, PREPOSITIONS

Firstly, complex prepositions are phrases, which means there are two or more words to them.

B2 example:

In addition to imitation,  there are a number of other procedures designed to give the therapist some degree of control over the child‘s language production.

  • The Louvain EAP dictionary: ‘in addition to’ is used to add new information and introduces the point to which you want to add something new.

C1 example:

In those few weeks, our lives had reverted back to what they‘d been prior to the murder of Sean Nokes.

listen

  • before a particular time or event

Prepositional complexity is found at different levels on the English Grammar Profile and English Vocabulary Profile.

Point 15 C1 in PREPOSITIONS is defined as:

less frequent complex prepositions, often in a more formal, literary or business context.

The two examples given are in the EGP as a guide:

ahead_II21 of_II22
on_II31 account_II32 of_II33

They both contain what ‘text inspector’ defines as B1 within them.

Next, it’s worth noting that this is the only C1 entry for prepositions and that there are no higher C2 entries for this category. In other words, this is the most complexity for prepositions listed on the English Grammar Profile.  Yet there are ‘multi’ part ones listed on the EVP at various levels, which makes grammar a hard way to choose which level each belongs to.

Point 13 B2 in the category of PREPOSITIONS is defined as:

wide range of complex prepositions.

  • (except|apart|as)  (for|from)
    (in)  (addition|spite)  (to|of)

Point 10 B1 in PREPOSITIONS is defined as:

an increasing range of complex prepositions.

The examples listed include:

(instead|out|outside|due)_II21 (of|to)_II22
(up|thanks) to

Point 5 A2 in Prepositions is defined:

a limited range of complex prepositions.

And the example sentences when coded are:

(in)_II31 (front)_II32 (of)_II33
(and)_RR31 (so)_RR32 (on)_RR33
(such)_II21 (as)_II22
(each)_PPX221 (other)_PPX222
(near)_II21 (to)_II22
