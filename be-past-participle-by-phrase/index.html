BE + past participle + BY phrase

A2, participle, PASSIVES, VERBS

The structure “BE + past participle + BY” is commonly used in English to form passive voice sentences.

In the passive voice, the subject of the sentence is acted upon by some other agent. The agent can be specified with a “by” phrase, but it’s not always necessary.

Here’s how it works:

BE: This is a form of the verb “to be”. It can be in any tense (am, is, are, was, were, been) and must agree with the subject of the sentence.

Past Participle: This is the past participle form of the main verb in the sentence. The past participle of a verb is typically formed in English by adding “-ed” to the end of the regular verbs, but many common verbs are irregular and have special forms.

BY: This preposition introduces the agent – the person or thing that performs the action.

For example, in the sentence “The cake was eaten by John”, “was” is a form of “to be”, “eaten” is the past participle of “eat”, and “by John” tells us who performed the action.

This structure allows us to focus on the action and its impact, rather than on who or what performed the action.
