will have + PAST PARTICIPLE (future perfect)

assumption, B2, C1, formal, FUTURE, NEGATIVE, perfect, polite, simple

There are 5 formally related grammar points in the English Grammar Profile in the category of FUTURE/perfect simple with ‘will.’  Many of them overlap and at C1 they are very rare and hard to find in corpora.  An example from the iWeb corpus of the future perfect form used for a polite assumption about the present:

I’d better stop here,

 but I hope I will have persuaded at least some people that there’s some reasonably low-hanging fruit around, 

at least for the time being.

Gowers’s Weblog

AFFIRMATIVE

B2 point 52 is defined as:

negative form

C1 point 59 is defined as:

make assumptions about the present, particularly with ‘you’.

C1 point 64 is defined as:

‘will’ as a politeness strategy, often in formal contexts.
