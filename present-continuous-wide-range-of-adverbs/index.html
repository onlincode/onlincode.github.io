present continuous + adverbs of indefinite frequency

ADVERBS, B2, C2, continuous, frequency, indefinite, PRESENT, range, surprising, undesirable

Let’s look at two expert examples of ‘BE + adverbs of indefinite frequency + VERBing‘:

At the time of application for Russian citizenship,
the spouses must be permanently residing in Russia 
and must be married for three years. 
  Russia Beyond – 16 Feb 2021
There are a few ways to approach loved ones 
who are persistently asking the same question. 
elmcroft.com
The first example above is a modal verb + BE + adverb + present participle and the second is an example of present continuous with an adverb in the mid position.

You should know that the most common adverbs of indefinite frequency include:

frequently, infrequently, regularly, always, usually, never, often, rarely, sometimes, seldom, once in a while, repeatedly, typically, hardly ever, and occasionally. 

You should already know about present continuous: ‘BE + verbING’.  Then, the strange thing about this grammar point is that it’s listed as C2 in the English Grammar Profile because there are some more very rare indefinite frequency adverbs you have probably never used before with it in a sentence.  In the category of PRESENT continuous:

a wide range of adverbs of indefinite frequency,

often to talk about surprising or undesirable situations or events.

There are only two examples given in the EGP: ‘persistently’ and ‘permanently’.  Both of those do not exist in the top 1000 clusters in the iWeb corpus.  It should be clear that this is a very rare grammar/vocabulary combination.

In the English Vocabulary Profile, ‘permanently‘ is listed as B2 meaning: always and forever.  There is no listing for ‘persistently’.  ‘Persistent’ is listed as an adjective at C2 meaning continuing to do something in a determined way.  Also, note that the adverbs ‘always‘ or ‘constantly‘ with past continuous are considered C2.
