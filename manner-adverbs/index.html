manner adverbs and phrases

A2, ADVERBS, manner, modify

Manner adverbs and adverb phrases are linguistic tools used to modify the way an action or event occurs. They provide additional information about how an action is performed or how an event takes place. By adding manner adverbs or adverb phrases to a sentence, you can provide more details about the manner in which something happens.

  1. Manner Adverbs: Manner adverbs are single words that directly modify a verb, expressing how an action is performed. They answer the question “How?” or “In what manner?” Examples of manner adverbs include:
    • Quickly: He ran quickly to catch the bus.
    • Carefully: She handled the fragile vase carefully.
    • Loudly: The children laughed loudly at the funny joke.
    • Quietly: Please speak quietly in the library.
    • Happily: They danced happily at their wedding.
  2. Adverb Phrases: Adverb phrases are groups of words that act as adverbs to modify a verb, adjective, or another adverb. They can function in the same way as manner adverbs, but they consist of multiple words. Adverb phrases provide more specific or detailed information about the manner of an action. Examples of adverb phrases include:
    • In a hurry: She left room in a hurry.
    • With great care: He assembled the delicate machinery with great care.
    • At full speed: The car raced down the highway at full speed.
    • In complete silence: The audience listened in complete silence.

In the English Grammar Profile, A2 point 28 in the category of ADVERBS is defined as:

limited range of manner adverbs and adverb phrases to modify how something happens.

In the sentence:

After getting his new glasses,

John was finally able to see clearly 

and appreciate the beauty of the world around him.

 the word “clearly” is used to describe the manner in which John is able to see.  Here, “clearly” means that John’s vision is no longer blurred or obstructed; he can see things sharply and distinctly. This could be due to the corrective effect of his new glasses. So, John is now able to discern or perceive visual details that he might have missed or seen as blurry before. This allows him to fully appreciate the visual aspects of his surroundings.

In a broader context, “seeing clearly” can also be used metaphorically to indicate understanding or comprehension. For example, if someone “sees a situation clearly,” it means they fully understand what’s happening.
