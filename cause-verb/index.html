CAUSE + noun phrase + TO infinitive

B2, causative, infinitive, justify, reason, VERBS

B2 To cause something means to make it happen.  This is often a bad thing/negative. Here are some advanced causative examples :

 

I think the jury is entitled to know what powerful force has caused him to change his mind.

cause to or ing | cause gerund or infinitive

A search in iWeb for infinitives after ’cause’:

CAUSE _TO *

We can see that “cause” is usually a noun if it is directly followed by an infinitive.

1 CAUSE TO BE 2573

God has spoken, proving your cause to be truthful and just.

Oh, you’ve got no cause to be encouraged, sir.

Why’d you boys go digging where you had no cause to be?

Well… He’s got no cause to be coming here like that.

You ain’t got no cause to be scared of nothing.

In the given examples, “cause to be” is used to indicate the reason or justification for a particular action or state of being. It is often used to describe a situation where something is brought into existence or made to happen.
