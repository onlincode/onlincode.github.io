himself | herself | myself | yourself

B1, emphasis, PREPOSITIONS, PRONOUNS, reflexive, singular

Well, I made a name for myself.

The Avengers

“for myself” is a prepositional phrase that acts as an adverbial modifier of the verb “made”. It consists of the preposition “for” and the reflexive pronoun “myself”, which refers back to the subject “I”.  This sentence is C2 because it also has an idiomatic meaning, which is to become famous or respected by a lot of people.

Here’s another C2 idiomatic expression, this time in the imperative form without the pronoun subject ‘you’:

Keep that to yourself.

TED

 

Well, I thought you must need some time to yourself.

A Kind of Murder

In this C1 example, ‘time to yourself’ means for ‘you’ only. ‘all’ can be used to emphasize this even more:

Maybe he wanted her all to himself.

Dismissed

Emphasis can be given with no words between the subject pronoun and the reflexive pronoun. Here is a B2 example:

To him, she herself is worthless.

The Art of the Steal

There are three very similar B1 points in the English Grammar Profile in the category of PRONOUNS/singular reflexive

Point 45 is defined as:

‘myself’, ‘yourself’, ‘himself’ and ‘herself’ after prepositions where the object of the preposition is the same as the subject of the verb.

Point 65 is:

‘myself’, ‘yourself’, ‘himself’ and ‘herself’ for emphasis.

Point 67 is:

‘myself’, ‘yourself’, ‘himself’ and ‘herself’ with ‘by’ to mean alone, without any help.

*note that ‘by itself’ is listed at B2
