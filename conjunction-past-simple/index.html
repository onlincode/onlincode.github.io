conjunction + past simple

B2, CONJUNCTIONS, PAST, polite, simple

In the English Grammar Profile, there are two points that overlap.  B2 point 55 is defined as:

PAST simple after ‘if’ as a politeness structure, especially in letters and emails.

B2 point 72 is defined as:

the past simple with a range of subordinating conjunctions, including ‘as soon as’, ‘before’, ‘if’, ‘once’, ‘since’, ‘so’, ‘until’, ‘when’, ‘while’

For example:

Can anyone guess what this building was used for a hundred years ago, 

before you went to school here, before I went to this school even?

listen

All the examples given in the EGP for point 55 follow the structure:

would  be (_rg) _JJ _CS * _VVD

A search in COCA:

2 WOULD BE GOOD IF WE TALKED 6

Sadly, iWeb corpus software does not allow the above search because it is too wide for their software.  Searching for collocates to the right of the first half is equally useless.
