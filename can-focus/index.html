CAN (focus)

C1, emphasis, fixed expressions, FOCUS, MODALITY

Fixed expressions are groups of words used together to express a particular idea or concept that is more specific than the individual words. They usually have literal meanings and do not change their word order or pattern. Here are some explanations for fixed expressions with the modal verb ‘can’ :

  • As you can see: This expression is used to draw attention to something that is visible or obvious to the listener or reader. For example, “As you can see, the graph shows a steady increase in sales.”
  • I can’t say: This expression is used to indicate that the speaker does not know the answer or does not want to reveal something. For example, “I can’t say when he will arrive. He didn’t tell me.”
  • I can tell you that: This expression is used to emphasize that the speaker is about to share some information or opinion. For example, “I can tell you that this cake is delicious. You should try it.”
  • As you can imagine: This expression is used to show sympathy or understanding for the listener’s situation or feelings. For example, “As you can imagine, she was very upset when she heard the news.”
  • I can say that: This expression is used to express certainty or confidence about something. For example, “I can say that this project will be completed on time. We have everything under control.”
  • I can assure you that: This expression is used to make a strong promise or guarantee about something. For example, “I can assure you that your money is safe with us. We have a secure system.”

In the English Grammar Profile, C1 point 186 in MODALITY is defined as:

 Expressions with ‘can’ or ‘can’t’ to give focus or add emphasis, such as ‘as you can see’, ‘I can’t say’, ‘I can tell you that’, ‘as you can imagine’, ‘I can say that’, ‘I can assure you that’

For example:

I can’t say I didn’t enjoy some of it.
