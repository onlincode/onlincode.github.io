-ise SUFFIX

suffix, VERBS

The “-ise” suffix is a common spelling variant of the verb-forming suffix “-ize.” It is primarily used in British English, while in American English, the corresponding spelling is “-ize.” The suffix “-ise” (or “-ize”) is added to the end of a noun or adjective to create a verb form.

For example,

real ⇒ realise

advert ⇒ advertise

organ ⇒ organise

Not all words ending in -ise are verbs though.  Here is a search in the iWeb corpus for:

*ise

The 4 most common tags are:

(vvi): 213 infinitives
(vv0): 127 base verbs
(jj): 119 adjectives
(nn1): 106 singular nouns
