past simple affirmative

A1, A2, affirmative, B1, PAST, simple, VERBS

We use the past simple affirmative to confirm situations and events that occurred and finished or were repeated in the past. For example, ‘I played soccer yesterday’ or ‘She studied hard for the exam’.

There are 3 entries in the English Grammar Profile for past simple affirmative range.  These points are best covered by the English Vocabulary Profile since it offers much more detail about vocabulary.

A1 point 1 is defined:

past simple affirmative form with a limited range of regular and irregular verbs.

A2 point 5 in the category of PAST/ simple:

affirmative form with an increasing range of verbs

Here’s an example of a past simple verb at A2 but with academic collocation:

I added some information.

(listen to this sentence)

B1 point 23

affirmative form with a wide range of verbs

The question is then why not include B2 C1 C2 range points.  Here is the most common B2 past simple verb, which is also in the Academic Word List:

Was anyone with you when you purchased the tickets?
